Synopsis
The story begins with Harry Potter, an orphan mistreated by his aunt and uncle Vernon Dursley. On his eleventh birthday, his life changes when he discovers that he is a wizard destined to attend the prestigious Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. In this magical place, he befriends Hermione Granger, a brilliant witch born to Muggle (non-magical) parents, and Ron Weasley, from a large family of wizards. Together, they experience the joys and challenges of learning magic while confronting the dark plans of Lord Voldemort, an exceptionally powerful dark wizard responsible for Harry's parents' death. Over the seven books, Harry and his friends grow, learn to master their powers, and face trials with courage and determination in an epic struggle between good and evil.
Interesting Information
Translation
The Harry Potter series has been translated into 80 languages, including Latin and Ancient Greek, making it one of the most translated book series in history.
Books
The seven books in the series have sold over 500 million copies worldwide, making it the best-selling book series of all time.
Publishing House
J.K. Rowling was initially rejected by 12 publishing houses before Bloomsbury agreed to publish the first book.
Last Book
The last book in the series, "Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows," broke records by becoming the fastest-selling book in history, with 11 million copies sold within the first 24 hours of its release in the United States and the United Kingdom.
Theme Park
The series inspired a theme park, "The Wizarding World of Harry Potter," in several Universal Studios parks around the world.

Harry Potter's Legacy
Harry Potter's influence on popular culture is undeniable. The saga has inspired merchandise, video games, exhibitions, and, as you mentioned, the theme park "The Wizarding World of Harry Potter" in several Universal Studios parks, allowing fans to literally immerse themselves in the wizarding world. Harry Potter's legacy continues through new generations of readers and viewers who continue to discover and appreciate this magical and timeless work. The impact on youth literature and the renewed interest in reading among young people are also important aspects to highlight.
A Rich and Complex Universe
One of Harry Potter's strengths lies in the richness and complexity of its universe. J.K. Rowling created a coherent and detailed magical world, with its own rules, history, fantastical creatures, and iconic places such as Diagon Alley, the Forbidden Forest, and the village of Hogsmeade. Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, with its four houses (Gryffindor, Slytherin, Hufflepuff, and Ravenclaw), potion classes, spells, and transfiguration, is at the heart of this universe and provides an unforgettable setting for learning and adventure.
